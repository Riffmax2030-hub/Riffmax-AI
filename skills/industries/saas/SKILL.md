# SaaS landing page patterns

## Section flow

1. **Hero** — clear product category line ("Project management for engineering teams"), benefit-led headline ("Ship 30% faster without process overhead"), demo screenshot or animated UI mockup. Two CTAs: primary "Start free" / "Try it free", secondary "Watch 2-min demo" or "Book a call".
2. **Logo wall** — 6–10 customer logos in a single row, grayscale. "Trusted by teams at..." Builds credibility immediately.
3. **Three-pillar features** — three benefit-oriented sections (not feature-oriented). Each pillar has: icon/illustration, benefit headline, 2-line explanation, "learn more" link.
4. **Deeper feature explainer** — one feature gets a longer treatment with screenshots and a real use case. Pick the most differentiated feature, not the most common.
5. **Social proof** — 1–2 case studies or testimonials with real names, real titles, real company logos. Numbers if you have them.
6. **Pricing teaser or comparison** — show pricing if simple, otherwise tease tiers and link to /pricing.
7. **Final CTA** — restate the headline, restate the CTA, add a "no credit card required" reassurance.

## Tone of voice

- Direct, technical, evidence-based. SaaS buyers are skeptical of marketing fluff.
- Speak the buyer's language. If selling to engineers, use "deploy", "ship", "PR", "incidents". If selling to ops, use "process", "throughput", "approvals".
- Quantify everything. "Faster" is weak. "30% fewer support tickets" is strong.
- Active voice. Short sentences. No marketing-ese ("revolutionary", "best-in-class", "next-generation").
- Show, don't tell — screenshots and demos do more than adjectives.

## Visual cues

- Color palette: neutral background (white or near-white), one strong accent color (blue, indigo, violet, green, or coral), grayscale UI screenshots with selective color highlights.
- Typography: modern geometric sans (Inter, Geist, Manrope) for everything. Big hero font (60–80px desktop), tight line-height (~1.05).
- Generous whitespace. Cards with thin borders, soft shadows. Rounded corners (12–16px).
- Product screenshots should show actual UI, ideally with realistic data, not Lorem Ipsum.
- Subtle gradients in hero backgrounds are acceptable but should be muted (not rainbow).

## Copy patterns

- Headlines under 8 words.
- Benefit > feature. "Cut deploy time in half" beats "GitHub Actions integration".
- Use brackets or contrasts: "Built for X, not Y" / "Less Z, more A".
- Pricing reassurance is critical: "Free for up to 5 users", "No credit card required", "Cancel anytime".
- Proof points near every claim. If you say "fast", show benchmark numbers nearby.

## Things to avoid

- Stock photos of people pointing at screens. Use product UI instead.
- "AI-powered" without saying what the AI does. Buyers see through this.
- Pricing tables hidden behind "Talk to sales" — only acceptable for true enterprise tier, never the entry tier.
- Three-card feature sections that all say the same thing in different words.
