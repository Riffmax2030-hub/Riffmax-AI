# Restaurant landing page patterns

## Section flow

1. **Hero** — large food photography (the actual signature dish), restaurant name displayed prominently, location + cuisine type subtitle. CTAs: "Reserve a table" (primary), "View menu" (secondary). Phone number visible.
2. **Welcome / story** — 1–2 short paragraphs introducing the restaurant: chef name, cuisine, what makes the place distinct.
3. **Menu highlights** — 4–8 featured dishes with photos, names in original language if relevant, English description, price. Don't show the full menu; tease.
4. **Atmosphere** — interior photos, candid customer shots if you have permission. Establishes the vibe (date-night vs. family vs. casual).
5. **Visit / hours** — address, hours, map, parking, "two blocks from X landmark", reservation link.
6. **Reviews** — 2–3 quoted reviews from customers or critics. Source matters: a quote from Eater is different from a Yelp review.
7. **Final CTA** — book a table (if the kind of place that takes reservations) or see today's specials.

## Tone of voice

- Sensory and inviting. Use words for taste, smell, atmosphere — not just adjectives like "delicious".
- Personality forward. A laid-back BBQ joint speaks differently than a tasting-menu fine-dining spot. Match the venue.
- Local voice — name neighborhoods, mention the city, reference local ingredients.
- Don't over-promise. "James Beard nominated" matters; "best burger in town" doesn't.

## Visual cues

- Color palette: warm — terracotta, deep red, mustard, forest green, cream. Or for upscale: dark navy, gold accents, ivory. Avoid stark white.
- Typography: editorial serif headlines (Lora, Cormorant, EB Garamond) or display script for the restaurant name. Sans for body.
- Big food photos. The food is the product — photos must be real, well-lit, appetizing.
- Don't be afraid of dark backgrounds for upscale dining.

## Copy patterns

- Restaurant name: prominent on hero, repeated subtly in footer. Customers search for the name.
- Menu items: dish name in caps or larger, description in italics, price aligned right.
- Hours: simple format. Closed days noted.
- Reservation CTA should link directly to OpenTable / Resy / phone — don't add friction.

## Things to avoid

- Generic stock photos of food. Customers know what's local; they'll spot a fake.
- Auto-playing music or video.
- Hiding the address. The single most-clicked link on a restaurant site is "directions".
- Long bio/manifesto sections. Save for a separate /about page.
