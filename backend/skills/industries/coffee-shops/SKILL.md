# Coffee shop landing page patterns

## Section flow

1. **Hero** — full-bleed photo of brewing or pour-over, location and hours overlaid. Headline names the experience, not the product (e.g. "Slow mornings, on tap" rather than "Specialty coffee"). Two CTAs: primary "Visit us" or "Order online", secondary "See our menu".
2. **Today's coffee** — current featured single-origin or blend with origin notes (region, altitude, tasting notes).
3. **Menu highlights** — 3–6 signature drinks/foods with subtle photography. Don't list the full menu; tease.
4. **Story / About** — short paragraph on the founders, sourcing philosophy, or roastery date. Include one staff or founder photo.
5. **Visit** — address, hours, map embed or styled map illustration. Mention parking, public transit, or "we're around the corner from X".
6. **Final CTA** — email signup for "first-batch alerts" or "drop-in hours".

## Tone of voice

- Warm, slightly poetic, never corporate.
- Short sentences. Sensory words: "pull", "bright", "roast date", "single-origin".
- Use the seasons, the time of day, the weather. Coffee is a ritual, treat copy like ritual.
- Avoid generic adjectives like "best", "premium", "amazing". Use specifics: "harvested in Yirgacheffe last December".
- First-person plural ("we roast") feels right; first-person singular ("I started this") works for solo founders.

## Visual cues

- Color palette: warm earth tones — espresso brown, cream, terracotta, deep forest green. Avoid bright primaries.
- Typography: a friendly serif for headlines (Fraunces, DM Serif, Playfair) paired with a clean sans for body (Inter, Manrope). One display font max.
- Plenty of negative space. Grid-aligned but feels handmade.
- Images dominate over text. If there's no photo, use a textured cream background, not flat white.
- Subtle grain or paper texture on backgrounds is acceptable.

## Copy patterns

- Hero headlines work as fragments, not sentences: "Coffee with a passport.", "Brewed slow. Served warm."
- "About us" is short. 3 sentences max in the hero version; full version on a separate page.
- Origin notes for any coffee drink should include: region, farm or co-op (when known), altitude, tasting notes (3 words max).
- Hours: list weekdays simply, don't make a 7-row table for "8–6, 8–6, 8–6, 8–6, 8–6, 9–4, 9–4". Group: "Weekdays 8–6, Weekends 9–4."

## Things to avoid

- Stock photos of latte art that look obviously shot in a studio. Use real local photos or skip.
- "Award-winning". Show the award; don't claim it.
- Loyalty program lock-ins as the primary CTA. Visitors haven't tried the coffee yet.
- Long scrolls with no rhythm. Coffee shops are calm; the site should feel calm.
